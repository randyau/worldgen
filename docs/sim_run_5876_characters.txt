WORLD ENGINE — 5876-YEAR SIMULATION RUN
Character Narratives (25 Sample Characters)
Generated from world.db event log
============================================================

Characters are drawn from across the full time span to represent the range of
character types: founders, long-reigning rulers, short-reigning rulers, and
specialists (physicians, scholars). Character ages are given in seasons
(15 seasons/year, so ageSeason 60 = 4 years; ageSeason 1962 = ~131 years).

============================================================
EARLY ERA (Years 1–100)
============================================================

--- 1. TURG "WARBORN" (Entity 68) ---
Born: Year 1 | Died: Year 10 | Civilization: Turg's Domain (Civ 19)
Ambition: 0.63 | Aggression: 0.85

The first war in the simulation was Turg's. Born Year 1 with the epithet
"Warborn" and extraordinary aggression, Turg founded the settlement Stonemoor
at [155,89] and immediately attracted hostility from neighbor Morda across
the river at [155,88]. By Year 3, Morda had declared a personal rivalry.
Turg returned the favor in Year 4.

In Year 8, Turg formally declared war on Morda's Domain, citing a "character
encounter." What followed was the simulation's most chaotic early conflict: in a
single year, both Stonemoor and Morda's capital Flintshore exchanged hands
multiple times. Turg raided Flintshore repeatedly (damage 17–28 per strike),
Morda counter-raided Stonemoor, and by Year 9 both settlements had hit
negative health, been "conquered," re-garrisoned, and lost again. The two
settlements traded ownership four times in two years.

Morda ultimately prevailed. By Year 9 Stonemoor fell for good — surviving
population 30. The war ended in Year 10 with "reason: surrender." Turg died
of old age in Year 10, at ageSeason 157 (~10 years). His domain collapsed
the same year. He never held a second settlement; he never made peace.


--- 2. MORDA "SHIELDBREAKER" (Entity 72) ---
Born: Year 1 | Died: Year 14 | Civilization: Morda's Domain (Civ 10)
Ambition: 0.14 | Aggression: 0.75

Morda "Shieldbreaker" won the war. Despite having the lowest ambition of any
simulation founder (0.14 — she apparently had no strategic goals, only a
talent for violence), Morda drove Turg's Domain to collapse and absorbed
Stonemoor. She founded Flintshore at [155,88] in Year 1 and held it through
the brutal exchange-conquest war of Year 8–10.

After the war, Morda ruled over two settlements in uneasy peace. She died
in Year 14 at ageSeason 224, having never declared another war. Her successor,
Proga, inherited a domain already weakened from the fighting — Morda's Domain
would collapse in Year 31, 17 years after her death. She won her war and it
did not matter.


--- 3. ELIAN "THE SWIFT" (Entity 71) ---
Born: Year 1 | Died: Year 12 | Civilization: Elian's Domain (Civ 9)
Ambition: 0.31 | Aggression: 0.49

Elian was the quietest founder. He established Highreach at [94,134], passed
succession to Ilaenith in Year 12 just before dying of old age (ageSeason 190),
and left behind what would become one of the simulation's most durable
civilizations. He declared no wars, formed no alliances, engaged in no
recorded conflicts. His 12 years of rule were peaceful, possibly because his
neighbors were equally overwhelmed with founding settlements.

His legacy was contested — Highreach was later conquered by Ilaenith, then
recaptured. The civilization that bears his name fought brutal wars in its
early decades that he never saw coming.


--- 4. CAELEN "WINDWALKER" (Entity 83) ---
Born: Year 1 | Died: Year 68 | Civilization: Caelen's Domain (Civ 8)
Ambition: 0.24 | Aggression: 0.34

Caelen was the longest-lived member of the founding generation, reaching
ageSeason 1080 (~68 years). He watched his civilization conquered and
reconquered across his entire adult life. By Year 5 he had already formed a
personal rivalry with Ilaenith of Elian's Domain. By Year 12, Ilaenith declared
war. By Year 14, both of Caelen's Domain's main settlements — Sharpkeep and
Deepreach — had been seized.

What makes Caelen remarkable is endurance. He survived both conquests, survived
a second war in Year 26–29 in which Elian's Domain again stripped all his
settlements, and lived to see a truce in Year 29. He was personally targeted
by Elian's Domain rulers for decades — AllianceFormed events show 8 different
rulers of Elian's Domain declaring personal rivalry against him across 40 years.

He died at last in Year 68, leaving his half-ruined civilization to his
successor Aelindra. His epithet "Windwalker" fits: he bent but never broke.


--- 5. ILAENITH "THE PATIENT" (Entity 130) ---
Born: Year 4 | Died: Year 126 | Civilization: Elian's Domain (Civ 9)
Ambition: 0.81 | Aggression: 0.54

Ilaenith lived 122 years (ageSeason 1962), the longest reign in the early-era
record. She succeeded Elian in Year 12 and immediately went to war against
Caelen's Domain, declaring conflict in Year 12 (the same year she took power).
She was the aggressor: conquering Sharpkeep in Year 14, Deepreach in Year 14,
then attacking again in Year 26 when Caelen's Domain had partly recovered.

Her second war was even more thorough. By Year 28, all of Caelen's Domain's
settlements — Sharpkeep, Deepreach, Oldmark, Cragmere — had been taken. A peace
truce in Year 29 ended the formal war, but Ilaenith continued declaring personal
rivalries with Caelen for years afterward, suggesting she regarded him as
unfinished business.

After the wars, Ilaenith's record becomes quiet. She ruled for 94 more years
following the Year 29 truce with no further wars. She died in Year 126, the
same year she transferred power to her successor Haelwyn. Her epithet "the
Patient" is ironic: she was patient in the sense of outlasting everyone, but
"the Conqueror" would have fit better.


--- 6. YRUG "BONECRUNCHER" (Entity 128) ---
Born: Year 4 | Died: Year 20 | Civilization: Idris's Domain (Civ 3)
Ambition: 0.45 | Aggression: 0.58

Yrug was the second ruler of Idris's Domain, succeeding founder Idris in Year 7.
Within 3 years he had declared war on Proga's Domain and was actively raiding
its capital Stonehollow [140,57]. The raids were systematic: Year 11–13 saw
repeated strikes (damage 14–27) that reduced Stonehollow's health to 2, then -17.
In Year 13 Stonehollow fell — survivingPop 30 — and became an Idris's Domain
settlement.

Yrug also faced a war from Crug's Domain (Civ 5), which declared against him
in Year 12, but this conflict produced no conquest and ended in truce by Year 16.
Yrug died in Year 20 at ageSeason 258, having added one settlement to the domain
in 13 years of rule. His successor continued Idris's Domain for another 416 years.


--- 7. BESSA "THE HAWK" (Entity 298) ---
Born: Year 12 | Died: Year 16 | Civilization: Caelen's Domain (Civ 8)
Ambition: 0.63 | Aggression: 0.76

Bessa lived only 4 years (ageSeason 76), but was one of the most destructive
characters per year of life in the simulation. Born in Year 12, she immediately
founded Sharpkeep [90,134] for Caelen's Domain, then turned raider — attacking
Elian's Domain settlements while Ilaenith was simultaneously attacking hers.

In Year 14–15, Bessa conquered two Elian's Domain settlements in counter-raids
(Sharpkeep and Cragmere), briefly restoring Caelen's Domain's territory. By
Year 15 she had conquered Cragmere [91,134] with survivingPop 39. She died in
Year 16, leaving behind a civ in the middle of active war. Her brief life was
entirely military — born, fight, conquer, die.


--- 8. FAENITH "OF THE LONG WATCH" (Entity 514) ---
Born: Year 21 | Died: Year 123 | Civilization: Elian's Domain (Civ 9)
Ambition: 0.70 | Aggression: 0.47

Faenith lived 102 years (ageSeason 1640) and spent most of them raiding Caelen's
Domain settlements during the prolonged second Elian-Caelen war (Years 26–29).
He founded Ironshore [93,133] in Year 21 and immediately joined the war effort,
executing at least 4 conquests of Caelen's Domain territories (Oldmark twice,
Cragmere) during the Year 26–28 campaign.

After the war ended in Year 29, Faenith appears to have lived peacefully for
94 years. He died in Year 123, outlasting Ilaenith by 3 years. His settlement
Ironshore was eventually abandoned in Year 273. His epithet "of the Long Watch"
suits him well — a long life of waiting between the bursts of early violence.


--- 9. AELINDRA "STARBORN" (Entity 620) ---
Born: Year 26 | Long reign | Civilization: Caelen's Domain (Civ 8)
Ambition: 0.48 | Aggression: 0.67

Aelindra inherited Caelen's Domain from its founder in Year 68, when Caelen died
after watching his civilization repeatedly conquered. She had been fighting before
she was ruler — raiding Elian's Domain settlements as early as Year 26 (Year 27:
conquered Oldmark with survivingPop 21). After taking the throne she continued
as a target: 8 different Elian's Domain rulers declared personal rivalry against
her over the following decades.

What Aelindra represents is persistence: the Caelen's Domain that technically
survives to Year 5876 is arguably her civilization more than Caelen's, since
she held it together through the post-founder collapse phase. She appears still
alive and active in Year 75 based on the rivalry declarations. Her eventual
death date is not captured in the sampled window, but she lived well past Year 68.


--- 10. ULRIC THE SCHOLAR (Entity 1924) ---
Born: ~Year 79 | Died: Year 83 | Civilization: Arlen's Domain (Civ 6)
Role: Scholar

Ulric is the first Scholar in the simulation record. He was appointed to the
scholar role in Year 79 at a settlement [91,93] that had just crossed the
population threshold of 300, then made his discovery in Year 80. He died in
Year 83 at ageSeason 60 (~4 years).

There is almost nothing else to say about Ulric. He was born old (appointed
to specialist role immediately at life start), made one discovery, and died.
The scholar discovery event (Type 3304) captures no content — just his name
and location. What he discovered, what it meant, what anyone thought of it —
none of that is recorded. He is a placeholder for a life.

This is the central narrative problem: the simulation generates the fact of
a Scholar Discovery but not its meaning.

MIDDLE ERA (Years 100–2000)

--- 11. ZORAN THE PHYSICIAN (Entity 7096) ---
Appointed: Year 256 | Died: Year 260 | Civilization: Lrug's Domain (Civ 17)

Zoran was appointed Physician in Year 256 at a settlement [139,96] that had
just reached population 500. He healed multiple patients across Years 256–260
(at least 9 distinct healing events), treating recurring patients including
individuals 5239 and 5859 over multiple consecutive years. He died in Year 260
at ageSeason 60 — the physician lifespan cap.

What Zoran's record reveals: the Physician role is entirely reactive. He was
spawned when the settlement hit a threshold, healed the same patients repeatedly,
and died after exactly 4 years of service regardless of outcome. The healing
events show "healed: 10" for each patient interaction — there is no recovery
arc, no crisis, no resolution. He healed people, they may or may not have
survived, and he died.


--- 12. ERYN THE PHYSICIAN (Entity 10098) ---
Appointed: Year 354 | Died: Year 358 | Civilization: Lrug's Domain (Civ 17)

Eryn followed the same pattern as Zoran exactly — appointed Year 354 at [138,96],
treated recurring patients (9547, 8672, 9983, 10108) for 4 years, died at
ageSeason 60. There is nothing to distinguish Eryn from Zoran except names
and patient IDs.

This interchangeability is a design signal: all Physicians are functionally
identical in the simulation. There is no variation in their stories. For
narrative generation purposes, they need differentiated contexts — was there
a disease outbreak when they were appointed? Did their patients survive? Did
the settlement's population grow or shrink under their care?


--- 13. ILYAREL "THE DREAMER" (Entity 22456) ---
Born: Year 757 | Died: Year 873 | Civilization: Arlen's Domain (Civ 6)
Ambition: 0.58 | Aggression: 0.86

Ilyarel "the Dreamer" ruled Arlen's Domain for 116 years and declared 9 wars —
all against Pella's Domain, all citing "border_tension," all ending in truce
~4 years later. The pattern was exact: war Year 769, truce Year 773; war
Year 783, truce Year 787; war Year 797, truce Year 801; and so on, every 14
years until his death.

The epithet "the Dreamer" contrasts vividly with a character whose entire
existence in the record consists of border wars. Perhaps the name refers to
the dream of finally winning — a dream that 9 wars and 116 years never
fulfilled. He died in Year 873 having changed nothing about the Arlen/Pella
balance. His successor Kaelith took the throne and resumed the wars.

The tragedy of Ilyarel (if the simulation can produce tragedy) is not that he
failed at war — it's that the wars were structurally pointless from the start.
No raid events appear in his record; the wars are declared and then resolved
without any territorial change. The conflict was purely ceremonial.


--- 14. KAELITH "OF THE SILVER WOOD" (Entity 25714) ---
Born: Year 866 | Died: Year 954 | Civilization: Arlen's Domain (Civ 6)
Ambition: 0.90 | Aggression: 0.86

Kaelith had the highest ambition in the sample (0.90) and near-maximum
aggression. He ruled for 88 years and declared 6 wars against Pella's Domain.
Like Ilyarel, every war ended in truce; unlike Ilyarel, Kaelith's wars came
in shorter cycles (10–14 years). He achieved nothing that Ilyarel had not
already failed to achieve.

What Kaelith's record reveals about the simulation: ambition and aggression
scores do not create differentiated rulers. High ambition + high aggression
produces only more frequent wars against the same target. There is no
mechanism for a ruler to translate ambition into strategic gain.


--- 15. WROG "BRAVESCAR" (Entity 27551) ---

Wrog ruled Pella's Domain for 3 years (Year 935–938) and died at ageSeason 205

Wrog represents the many short-reign rulers in the high-succession-rate civs:

--- 16. SORVAEL "OF THE FIRST AGE" (Entity 33798) ---
Born: Year 1133 | Died: Year 1250 | Civilization: Pella's Domain (Civ 18)
Ambition: 0.33 | Aggression: 0.79

Sorvael ruled for 117 years (Year 1133–1250) and broke a pattern: he was one
of the Pella rulers who turned aggressor, declaring war on Arlen's Domain in
Year 1241 rather than waiting to be attacked. Despite low ambition, his
aggression drove him to action eventually. The war ended in truce in Year 1245,
4 years later. He died in Year 1250 at ageSeason 1884 (~125 years of life).

His epithet "of the First Age" is curious — he lived twelve centuries after
the founding. Either the simulation applies epithets without historical context,
or "First Age" refers to something within his culture's mythology that the
simulation doesn't record.


--- 17. QUINCE "THE WARMHEARTED" (Entity 41011) ---
Born: Year 1370 | Died: Year 1391 | Civilization: Arlen's Domain (Civ 6)
Ambition: 0.90 | Aggression: 0.82

Quince "the Warmhearted" declared war within 14 years of taking power and died
21 years into his reign. His epithet is the most ironic in the sample. Maximum
ambition, high aggression, warmhearted only by name. He ruled from Year 1374
to 1391, a reign of 17 years, and the only thing the record shows him doing
is declaring war on Pella's Domain in Year 1388 — which ended in truce 3 years
later under his successor's management.

Quince is a good example of the epithet disconnect: character epithets are
generated from some internal attribute, but the events they actually participate
in often contradict the flavor the epithet implies.

This second Ilyarel "the Dreamer" (the name and epithet recycle across

--- 25. ELOWEN THE PHYSICIAN (Entity 141211) ---
Appointed: Year 4656 | Died: Year 4660 | Civilization: Arlen's Domain (Civ 6)

Elowen served at [93,91] in the late era, treating patients 140437, 141126,

What Elowen's existence at Year 4656 tells us: the simulation has been running

centuries before. Without external change sources — wars, catastrophes, unique
events — the specialist characters are fully mechanical. They require substantial
narrative enrichment to become interesting to a reader.

============================================================
KEY PATTERNS AND OBSERVATIONS
============================================================

1. PHYSICIAN UNIFORMITY: All 6 physicians sampled are structurally identical.
   Appointed at population threshold, serve for exactly 4 years (ageSeason 60),
   heal recurring patients, die. No variation in outcome, no differentiation by
   context. For narrative generation, physicians need surrounding context to
   generate interesting stories: Was there a major outbreak when they were
   appointed? Did the settlement grow or shrink? Did their primary patient
   survive or die?

2. THE ARLEN/PELLA WAR CYCLE IS THE DOMINANT RULER NARRATIVE: Every Arlen ruler
   and most Pella rulers have the same story: declared war, truce, repeat. The
   character traits (ambition, aggression) generate different frequencies of war
   declaration but no qualitatively different outcomes. Rulers with 0.90 ambition
   do the same thing as rulers with 0.33 ambition. The war mechanic needs
   resolution conditions that can permanently end conflicts, or alternate
   outcomes that make different rulers feel distinct.

3. CHARACTER DEATHS: Overwhelmingly "old age." Only one character in the 25-sample
   died of disease. No character died in battle, despite 93 BattleOccurred events
   in the full log. The BattleOccurred event fires, but apparently does not
   produce character deaths. This is a gap: wars should have mortal consequences
   for the rulers who wage them.

4. SPECIALIST LIFESPANS ARE BROKEN: Physicians die at ageSeason 60 regardless of
   when they were born. If a physician is born as an adult (age 30) and lives to
   60 seasons, that's 4 years of life. Normal characters live to ageSeason 1000–2000.
   Physicians either spawn as very old adults, or the lifespan cap for specialists
   is capped at 60 seasons. Either way, the effective "career" is 4 years and they
   die immediately after. This should be a lifespan of normal adult duration.

5. NAME RECYCLING CREATES CONFUSION: "Ilyarel the Dreamer" appears twice in the
   sample across different centuries in the same civ. "Raelvael" rules three civs
   simultaneously at year 5876. "Caelen," "Aelindra," "Taelith" recycle across
   centuries. For narrative generation, recurring names will be confusing —
   readers cannot track lineages when names repeat. A historical deduplication
   or name-suffix system (Ilyarel II, Ilyarel the Younger) is needed.

6. EVENTS CAPTURE FACTS, NOT MEANING: The Scholar Discovery event exists. It
   fires once (Ulric, Year 80). It records who, where, when. Not what. Every
   specialist event (PhysicianHealed, ScholarDiscovery, MerchantTrade) captures
   mechanical outcomes without semantic content. For narrative generation to work,
   the event payloads need richer content: what was discovered, who was healed of
   what, what was traded. Otherwise the narrative layer has only "a thing happened
   here" to work with.

7. CHARACTER GOALS/ALLIANCES/GRIEF ARE ABSENT: The event types GoalFormed,
   GoalResolved, AllianceFormed (non-war), CharacterGrieved, CharacterFlourishing,
   CharacterSpiraling all exist in the enum but none appear in the 5876-year run
   (except AllianceFormed which appears only as rivalry declarations). These
   emotional arcs are entirely missing from the character record. Without them,
   every character's inner life is invisible.
