OVERVIEW
--------
Twenty civilizations were founded in Year 1. Six survived to the end of the
simulation (Year 5876). Fourteen collapsed within the first 436 years, most
within the first decade. The simulation records 20 unique rulers as founders,
229 wars declared, 1,770 settlements founded, 1,665 settlement abandonments,
75,570 disease outbreaks, and 9,522 wildlife raids across the entire run.

The dominant story of the late era is the endless war between Arlen's Domain
(Civ 6) and Pella's Domain (Civ 18), which repeated on a ~10-year cycle for
nearly the entire 5,876-year span.

============================================================
THE SIX SURVIVORS
============================================================

--- ARLEN'S DOMAIN (Civ 6) ---
Founded: Year 1 by Arlen, capital at Flintwatch [87,97]
Status at Year 5876: ACTIVE — 20 settlements, current ruler Samlo
Total settlements founded: 241 | Total rulers: 165 | Wars declared: 158

Arlen's Domain is the most militaristic civilization in the simulation.
Founded by Arlen in the wild central-western region, it passed to a succession
of rulers (Gorin, Merry, Lanny...) within the first 15 years as the founder
generation aged out. By the mid-first century the civ had settled into its
defining pattern: perpetual border war with Pella's Domain.

Every ruler of Arlen's Domain, without exception, declared war on Pella's
Domain, typically within 10-15 years of taking the throne, citing "border
tension." The wars lasted 4-6 years, ended in truce, and restarted under the
next reign. This cycle repeated 158 times across 165 rulers. None ever
achieved a decisive victory. None ever sued for permanent peace.

By Year 5876 the civ held 20 settlements in a dense band across the
western-central terrain. It had founded 241 settlements total, of which 221
were eventually abandoned — a constant churn of expansion and collapse driven
by disease (the region suffered tens of thousands of outbreaks) and wildlife
raids. The civ's current ruler, Samlo, took power in Year 5873.

Notable rulers: Ilyarel "the Dreamer" (757–873), who declared 9 wars across
his 116-year reign; Kaelith "of the Silver Wood" (866–954), maximum aggression
and ambition by trait; Quince "the Warmhearted" (1370–1391), who died within
21 years of taking power; Maewyn "Windwalker" (1775–1863).

Character note: The epithet "the Dreamer" appears twice in Arlen's Domain
across different rulers separated by centuries — names and epithets recycle.


--- PELLA'S DOMAIN (Civ 18) ---
Founded: Year 1 by Pella, capital at Redmill [81,69]
Status at Year 5876: ACTIVE — 21 settlements, current ruler Lirael
Total settlements founded: 786 | Total rulers: 179 | Wars declared: 65

Pella's Domain is the most expansionist civilization by raw numbers: 786
settlements founded over 5,876 years, more than any other civ. It is also the
most warred-upon — every ruler of neighboring Arlen's Domain declared war on
it repeatedly, and many of Pella's own rulers eventually returned the favor,
declaring 65 wars of their own against Arlen.

Founded by Pella in the northwestern terrain adjacent to Arlen's Domain, the
civ survived the early collapse wave through numbers — it was actively
expanding even as nine other civs fell in the first 40 years. Unlike Arlen,
which fought mostly to hold ground, Pella constantly founded and lost
settlements, cycling through them at a rate that suggests severe environmental
pressure: disease and wildlife raids destroyed settlements almost as fast as
new ones were built, particularly in the mid-region around [85, 65–75].

Some notable Pella rulers: Sorvael "of the First Age" (1133–1250), who ruled
for 117 years and eventually became an aggressor, declaring war on Arlen rather
than waiting to be attacked; Phaelen "Twilightborn" (1682–1804), who ruled
for 122 years and also turned aggressor; Faenith "the Undying" (2904–3010),
who ruled for 106 years with high ambition and aggression. Current ruler
Lirael took power in Year 5856 and declared war on Arlen in Year 5870.

The endless Arlen/Pella conflict never produced a decisive outcome. No
settlement was permanently conquered, no ruler succeeded in absorbing the
rival civ.


--- FAELINDRA'S DOMAIN (Civ 16) ---
Founded: Year 1 by Faelindra, capital at Brightgate [109,148]
Status at Year 5876: ACTIVE — 21 settlements, current ruler Raelvael
Total settlements founded: 436 | Total rulers: 173 | Wars declared: 0

Faelindra's Domain is the most peaceable of the survivors. Located in the
southern terrain [109–115, 140–150], it never appears as a war declarer in
the event log — not once across 5,876 years and 173 rulers. It simply grew.

The civ expanded outward from Brightgate through the highland approaches,
founding settlements in a slow but persistent spread. The southern terrain
appears more hostile than average (436 settlements founded, 415 abandoned),
but Faelindra's Domain always maintained a core of 20+ active settlements.
Its 173 rulers succeeded one another without apparent crisis — no succession
crises appear in the record, no wars, no conquests.

What drove the constant settlement abandonment? Disease and wildlife. The civ
logged thousands of disease outbreaks in its southern settlements, and the
highland terrain apparently hosted frequent beast pack activity. But without
wars consuming resources, the civ could continually rebuild.

The current ruler, Raelvael, took power in Year 5869.


--- ELIAN'S DOMAIN (Civ 9) ---
Founded: Year 1 by Elian, capital at Highreach [94,134]
Status at Year 5876: ACTIVE — 17 settlements, current ruler Raelvael
Total settlements founded: 72 | Total rulers: 182 | Wars declared: 2

Elian's Domain had a violent early history and a long peaceful plateau. Founded
by Elian "the Swift" in Year 1 at the coast-adjacent southern region [94,134],
it almost immediately fell into war with neighboring Caelen's Domain (Civ 8)
under Elian's successor Ilaenith. Ilaenith conquered both Caelen's Domain
settlements within 14 years, absorbed them briefly, lost them back, and
re-conquered them in a second war. By Year 29 the two civs had fought to
near-destruction; Caelen's Domain surrendered but survived.

After that early violence, Elian's Domain stopped initiating wars (only 2 total
in the record, both early). It held its 17 settlements with remarkable
stability through 182 rulers, enduring waves of disease and wildlife raids
without the constant war cycle that consumed Arlen and Pella. The late-era
succession rate was high — 7 rulers in the last 15 years — suggesting the
region's environmental stress led to short natural lifespans.

The current ruler, Raelvael, took power in Year 5873. (Note: "Raelvael" also
appears as the current ruler of Faelindra's Domain — name recycling across
civs is common in the simulation.)


--- LRUG'S DOMAIN (Civ 17) ---
Founded: Year 1 by Lrug, capital at Broadvale [171,96]
Status at Year 5876: ACTIVE — 21 settlements, current ruler Raelvael
Total settlements founded: 159 | Total rulers: 323 | Wars declared: 0

Lrug's Domain has the highest succession count of any civilization: 323 rulers
across 5,876 years. That is roughly one ruler every 18 years on average, with
many reigns far shorter. Founded by Lrug in the eastern terrain [171,96], the
civ passed to Taelith within 14 years and maintained an eastern frontier
through waves of rulers. Like Faelindra's Domain, it never declared a war —
not once in its entire history.

The rapid succession rate (nearly double any other civ) suggests either a very
hostile environmental context (short lifespans from disease), or something in
the eastern terrain's configuration that produced unusually brief reigns. The
civ is healthy at year 5876 (21 settlements), so the turnover didn't destabilize it.

Current ruler: Raelvael (third civ with this name at year 5876 — name
collision is increasingly common in the late simulation).


--- CAELEN'S DOMAIN (Civ 8) ---
Founded: Year 1 by Caelen, capital at Deepreach [92,134]
Status at Year 5876: ACTIVE — 3 settlements, technically surviving
Total settlements founded: 3 | Total rulers: 1 (founding generation only)

Caelen's Domain is the most improbable survivor. It was conquered in Year 14
(both settlements taken by Elian's Domain under Ilaenith), reconquered again
in Year 26–28 (all remaining settlements seized by Elian's Domain in a second
war), and formally collapsed in Year 138. It appears in the CivilizationCollapsed
event log multiple times under "reason: conquered."

Yet at year 5876, three settlements appear to carry its civId in the founding
records. These likely represent a rump state that was never formally absorbed —
the settlements Caelen's Domain founded but that were re-founded in its name
after conquests without a proper collapse being registered. It is effectively
a ghost civilization: 3 tiny settlements at the edge of Elian's Domain's sphere.

The founder, Caelen "Windwalker," was the longest-lived of the first generation
at 68 years (4 seasons/year = ~272 seasons lived to ageSeason 1080). He
watched his domain conquered and reconquered before dying of old age.

--- LYRA'S DOMAIN (Civ 15) ---
Founded: Year 1 by Lyra, capital at Broadmoor [133,78]
Collapsed: Year 4 — first civilization to fall

Lyra's Domain lasted only 4 years, the briefest run in the simulation. The
collapse event gives no reason beyond "CivilizationCollapsed." Given that
disease outbreaks began appearing in nearby settlements by Year 7 and many
first-generation civs faced immediate disease pressure, Lyra's Domain likely
lost its single settlement Broadmoor to disease or wildlife before it could
expand. It left no mark on the record.


--- ZOR'S DOMAIN (Civ 4), KIRA'S DOMAIN (Civ 11), CERYN'S DOMAIN (Civ 20) ---
Founded: Year 1 | Collapsed: Year 8

Three civs fell simultaneously in Year 8. Zor's Domain (Whitemere [162,66]),

outbreaks are recorded at Whitemere and Brightcliff in Year 7 — their single
settlements apparently collapsed under the infection before the civ could
expand or recover. Ceryn's Domain at Greenmere shared a tile with Idris's
Domain's settlement Coldfell — likely outcompeted or destroyed in the
crowded founding cluster.

--- IDRIS'S DOMAIN (Civ 3) ---
Founded: Year 1 by Idris, capital at Coldfell [138,58]
Collapsed: Year 436

Idris's Domain was the most durable of the collapsed civilizations, surviving
436 years and founding 56 settlements with 52 ruler successions. It began
aggressively — the second ruler, Yrug "Bonecruncher," declared war and
conquered Proga's Domain within 14 years — but the civ's history after Yrug
becomes quieter. It fought two wars total and built steadily before eventually
losing its last settlement around Year 436.

Its collapse is significant: the civ lasted four centuries, longer than the
entire existence of most of the failed civs combined. Yet it still fell, while
Arlen's Domain and Pella's Domain to its west endured indefinitely.

KEY PATTERNS AND OBSERVATIONS
============================================================

1. EARLY DEATH WAVE: 14 of 20 civs (70%) collapsed within 436 years, with 10
   falling in the first 31 years. The founding cluster Year 1 was probably
   too dense — many civs founded at the same tile cluster could not survive
   simultaneous disease pressure.

2. THE ARLEN/PELLA DEADLOCK: The simulation's dominant narrative for 5,800+
   years is the Arlen-Pella conflict — 158 wars with no decisive resolution.
   Every single ruler of Arlen's Domain declared war. Wars end in truce ~4
   years after declaration and restart ~10-15 years later. The war trigger
   "border_tension" fires mechanically when rulers have high aggression and
   are in proximity. No ruler has sufficient leverage to end the conflict
   permanently. This is the simulation's most urgent design problem.

3. DISEASE DOMINATES: 75,570 disease outbreaks across the run — roughly 13
   per year. With 20 active settlements per surviving civ, this means nearly
   every settlement is infected almost continuously. Disease is the primary
   cause of settlement abandonment (1,665 total) and likely the primary
   killer of characters (though death cause attribution is imperfect). The
   disease mechanic vastly overshadows war as a driver of settlement loss.

4. PEACEFUL CIVS SURVIVE JUST AS WELL: Faelindra's Domain and Lrug's Domain
   never declared a war and both thrive at year 5876 with 21 settlements each.
   Arlen's Domain declared 158 wars and also has ~20 settlements. Wars do not
   appear to produce any long-term advantage.

5. NAME RECYCLING IS HEAVY: By Year 5876, "Raelvael" is simultaneously the
   ruler of three different civilizations (Civs 6, 9, and 17). Common names
   ("Ilyarel," "Caelen," "Taelith," "Aelindra") repeat across centuries and
   civs. This is cosmetically jarring and will matter greatly when narrative
   generation references historical figures.

6. SETTLEMENT CHURN: Pella's Domain founded 786 settlements but only has 21
   at end — a 97% abandonment rate. Faelindra's Domain: 436 founded, 21
   surviving — 95% rate. This churn is almost entirely disease-driven.
