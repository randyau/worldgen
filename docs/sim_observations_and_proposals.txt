WORLD ENGINE — SIM OBSERVATIONS & PROPOSALS
Based on 5876-year run analysis
============================================================

This document proposes changes in two categories:
  A. Sim fixes — things that are actively broken or producing bad simulation output
  B. M3 prerequisites — features needed before narrative generation can work

============================================================
A. SIM FIXES (things broken right now)
============================================================

A1. PHYSICIAN LIFESPAN IS WRONG
------------------------------------------------------------
Observation: Every physician in the run (6 sampled, likely all 638 total) dies
at ageSeason 60 — roughly 4 years of life. Normal rulers live to ageSeason
1000–2000. This is because specialists appear to be spawned as "old" adults
already close to their death threshold, or the specialist lifespan cap is
set to 60.

Impact: Physicians have 4-year careers then die. This is absurd. A physician
should be a productive adult who lives a normal lifespan, practices medicine
for decades, and retires or dies of natural causes in old age.

Fix: Audit the specialist spawn logic. If specialists are born at high initial
age, change them to spawn at a normal young-adult age (ageSeason 50–100 out of
~1500). If there is a hard cap of 60 on specialist lifespan, remove it. The
specialist ageSeason at death should match the same distribution as non-rulers.


A2. DISEASE IS CATASTROPHICALLY OVERTUNED
------------------------------------------------------------
Observation: 75,570 disease outbreaks in 5,876 years = ~13 per year. With
~100 active settlements across all civs, this means each settlement is infected
~once per 8 years. But the DiseaseRecovered events (73,870) roughly match
outbreak events, so settlements cycle in and out of disease perpetually.
Settlement abandonment (1,665 events) is heavily driven by disease. Pella's
Domain founded 786 settlements and has 21 surviving — 97% abandonment rate.

Impact: Disease overshadows all other dynamics. Wars, exploration, succession,
characters — everything is dwarfed by the disease churn. The world feels like
a perpetual plague simulation, not a civilization builder.

Fix: Reduce disease spawn probability by 5–10x. The current rate produces
absurd churn. Target: each settlement is infected perhaps once every 50–100
years, not once every 8 years. Also investigate whether disease is the primary
driver of settlement abandonment — if so, reduce the abandon threshold so
settlements survive disease episodes rather than collapsing.


A3. WARS PRODUCE NO TERRITORIAL CHANGE AND NO CHARACTER DEATH
------------------------------------------------------------
Observation: Arlen's Domain declared 158 wars against Pella's Domain. Not a
single one produced a conquered settlement, a ruler death, or any lasting
territorial change. Every war ended in "truce" or "surrender" with the
status quo restored. The 15 SettlementConquered events in the entire run are
all from the first 30 years.

Impact: After Year 30, wars are purely ceremonial events. They generate
events, cost some raid damage, and resolve. No ruler has ever died in battle.
No settlement has changed hands in thousands of years. War as a mechanic has
become completely inert.

Fix (two parts):
  1. War resolution should sometimes produce settlement conquest or ruler death.
     A war that lasts 4+ years of raids against a weakened settlement should
     have a real chance of conquest rather than defaulting to truce.
  2. The BattleOccurred event should carry a risk of ruler death. A ruler with
     high aggression who has declared multiple wars should be at risk of dying
     in battle. Currently, rulers die only of old age or disease.


A4. THE WAR TRIGGER IS A ONE-TRICK LOOP
------------------------------------------------------------
Observation: Every single war between Arlen and Pella cites "border_tension"
as the cause. The war trigger fires mechanically every ~10-14 years regardless
of outcome. There is no de-escalation, no lasting peace, no alternate cause
that could break the cycle.

Impact: The primary narrative of the simulation's mature era is "Arlen declared
war on Pella. Again." This is narratively exhausting and uninteresting.

Fix:
  - Add a war exhaustion mechanic: after N consecutive truces with the same
    target, the declaring civ gets a cooldown period that scales up.
  - Add alternate outcomes: a decisive ruler death in battle, a great victory
    by conquest, or a formal peace treaty could end the Arlen/Pella conflict
    permanently (until a later ruler reignites it).
  - Add more war causes beyond border_tension and character_encounter.
    Succession crises, disease weakness, or resource scarcity should be able
    to trigger opportunistic attacks.


A5. CHARACTER EMOTIONAL STATE EVENTS NEVER FIRE
------------------------------------------------------------
Observation: GoalFormed, GoalResolved, AllianceFormed (non-war), CharacterGrieved,
CharacterFlourishing, CharacterSpiraling — zero occurrences in 5,876 years
(except rivalry declarations). These event types exist in the enum but are
never generated.

Impact: Character inner lives are completely absent. Every character is
externally defined by their faction role and wars. There is no grief, no joy,
no goal, no bond in the record. Narrative generation over this data will
produce shallow, factual accounts.

Fix: Audit why these events don't fire. Most likely the thresholds are never
met or the code paths that would emit them are not reached. Specific checks:
  - CharacterFlourishing (wellbeing > 0.7): is wellbeing tracked? Does any
    character ever hit 0.7?
  - CharacterGrieved: requires a "trusted companion" death — are companion
    bonds ever formed?
  - GoalFormed: what triggers goal formation? Are the trigger conditions met?


A6. EARLY-ERA OVERCROWDING KILLS 70% OF CIVILIZATIONS IN THE FIRST DECADE
------------------------------------------------------------
Observation: 10 of 20 civilizations collapsed within 31 years, 14 within 436
years. The founding cluster placed 20 civs in a small geographic area, all
starting simultaneously. Disease pressure and early competition destroyed most
before they could establish viable populations.

Impact: The simulation has a rich early-era history (Years 1–50) and a
monotonous late-era history (Years 50–5876 with the same 5-6 civs doing the
same things). The "world" generated feels empty by Year 100 — only a few civs
survive in a map designed for 20.

Fix: Reduce founding civ count, spread them more widely, or add survival bonuses
in the first 20 years (guaranteed minimum pop, disease immunity period, or a
founding grace period). Alternatively, embrace the die-off but make early
collapse narratively interesting (great founder stories, not just "disease").


============================================================
B. M3 PREREQUISITES (for narrative generation to work)
============================================================

B1. EVENT PAYLOADS NEED SEMANTIC CONTENT
------------------------------------------------------------
What's missing: Events capture facts (who, where, when, what type) but not
meaning (what was discovered, what was healed, what was traded, why the war
felt personal). The narrative generation layer has only raw facts to work with.

Required additions to event payloads for M3:
  - ScholarDiscovery: add `discoveryType` (medical, astronomical, agricultural,
    philosophical) and optionally `discoveryName` for legendary discoveries.
  - PhysicianHealed: add `illness` type and `outcome` (cured/managed/died anyway).
  - MerchantTradeCompleted: add `goods`, `tradePartner` settlement/civ.
  - ArtworkCreated: add `artType` (song, monument, painting) and `subject`.
  - WarDeclared / WarEnded: add `cause_description` (a human-readable phrase
    beyond the enum value) and `outcome_summary`.
  - BattleOccurred: add `outcome` (victory/defeat/draw) and `casualties` (bool).


B2. A SUMMARIZATION LAYER IS NEEDED FOR NARRATIVE RETRIEVAL
------------------------------------------------------------
What's missing: 598,980 events cannot be fed to a narrative system directly.
Narrative generation needs pre-aggregated character summaries and civ summaries.

Required for M3:
  - CharacterSummary table: for each character, precompute total wars declared,
    settlements founded, years lived, key events (first war, death cause,
    ruled which civ, succession from/to whom).
  - CivilizationSummary table: peak settlement count, total rulers, wars
    declared/received, first/last year, surviving status.
  - EraTag system: assign characters and events to named eras (Early Founding,
    The Collapse Years, The Long Peace, etc.) so narrative can reference eras
    by name rather than raw year numbers.


B3. NAME DISAMBIGUATION IS REQUIRED
------------------------------------------------------------
What's missing: Names recycle. "Raelvael" rules 3 civs simultaneously at
Year 5876. "Ilyarel the Dreamer" appears in two different centuries in the
same civ. "Caelen" is both the founder of Civ 8 (Year 1) and a ruler of
Pella's Domain (Year 3010).

Impact for M3: Any narrative reference to "Ilyarel" or "Caelen" is ambiguous.
A player asking "tell me about Caelen" could mean 10 different characters.

Required for M3:
  - Name disambiguation suffix: Caelen I, Caelen II, etc. (ordinal by birth year
    within the same name+epithet combination).
  - Or: epithet uniqueness enforcement — if "the Dreamer" is already taken in
    living memory, generate a different epithet.
  - At minimum: a query interface that returns a disambiguation list when a name
    refers to multiple characters.


B4. A CHARACTER LINEAGE / SUCCESSION GRAPH
------------------------------------------------------------
What's missing: The succession chain exists in event data (SuccessionOccurred)
but is not pre-indexed. To say "Ruler X was the 47th ruler of Arlen's Domain,
succeeding Ruler Y who had ruled for 30 years," a narrative system must
reconstruct the chain from raw events.

Required for M3:
  - Pre-index succession chains per civ into a linked list or adjacency table.
  - Track "generation number" (ordinal position in the succession line).
  - Track dynastic groups: consecutive rulers from the same family/bloodline
    (if family tracking is added) or at minimum consecutive rulers with
    similar traits.


B5. CAUSE-EFFECT CHAINS NEED SURFACE-LEVEL TAGGING
------------------------------------------------------------
What's missing: The CausalEdges table exists but is empty or unused. Events
that are causally linked (a disease outbreak causing settlement abandonment,
a war declaration causing a character's personal rivalry formation) are not
explicitly connected in the data.

Required for M3:
  - Populate CausalEdges for at least: disease → abandonment, war → battle →
    surrender/truce, succession crisis → instability events.
  - Add a "consequence" field to key events: "Arlen declared war on Pella;
    this led to 3 settlement raids and a truce 4 years later."
  - The narrative system needs to be able to say "because of X, Y happened"
    — not just enumerate events in year order.


B6. REGIONAL IDENTITY / CULTURAL DIFFERENTIATION
------------------------------------------------------------
What's missing: All 5 surviving civilizations are mechanically identical except
for location. Arlen's Domain has fought 158 wars; Faelindra's Domain has fought
0. But there is no in-world explanation for this difference — no cultural value
system, no terrain-based isolation, no resource scarcity that would explain
the behavioral divergence.

Required for M3 (or earlier):
  - Civilizations should develop traits over time based on their history:
    Arlen's Domain = "Warrior Culture" (high war frequency), Faelindra's Domain
    = "Expansionist Culture" (high settlement founding), Lrug's Domain =
    "Unstable Succession Culture" (high succession rate).
  - These traits should appear in the event record and be queryable by the
    narrative layer: "Arlen's Domain, known for its century-spanning conflict
    with Pella's Domain, has a tradition of warrior rulers..."
  - Even simple boolean tags (militaristic, expansionist, reclusive) would give
    the narrative layer something to work with.


B7. THE QUERY INTERFACE FOR NARRATIVE
------------------------------------------------------------
What's missing: There is no standard way for a narrative system to ask "tell
me about Civ 6" or "who was the most significant ruler of Pella's Domain in
Year 2000?" The event store is a flat log. Narrative generation requires
structured historical queries.

Required for M3:
  - A HistoryQuery API (in WorldEngine.Sim) that exposes:
      GetCivHistory(civId, startYear, endYear) → structured summary
      GetCharacterHistory(charId) → full event timeline
      GetSignificantEvents(yearRange, tier) → headline events for narrative
      GetRulerAtYear(civId, year) → who was ruler
      GetConflictHistory(civA, civB) → all wars between two civs
  - These queries should be pre-indexed (not recomputed from raw events on
    each call) given the 598,980-event dataset size.
  - This is the boundary between the sim (WorldEngine.Sim) and the narrative
    engine (M3 feature): the sim produces structured historical data; the
    narrative system consumes it to generate text.

PRIORITY ORDER
============================================================

IMMEDIATE (fix before more sim runs):
  1. A1 — Physician lifespan (fundamentally broken)
  2. A2 — Disease overtuning (dominates all other dynamics)
  3. A5 — Emotional event firing (characters are empty without these)

BEFORE M3 BEGINS:
  4. A3/A4 — War outcome and war exhaustion (wars need consequences)
  5. B1 — Richer event payloads (narrative layer needs content)
  6. B3 — Name disambiguation (required for coherent references)
  7. B4 — Succession chain indexing (needed for ruler narrative queries)

M3 PARALLEL WORK (can develop alongside narrative):
  8. B2 — Summarization layer
  9. B5 — Causal edge population
  10. B6 — Cultural trait tags
  11. B7 — HistoryQuery API
  12. A6 — Early-era survival tuning
